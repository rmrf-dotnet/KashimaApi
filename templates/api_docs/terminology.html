MTB Terminology

Mountain biking has its own language. Here's what you need to know.

Trail

A general-purpose mountain bike. Good for climbing and descending on mixed terrain.

Enduro

Designed for rough, aggressive riding. More travel than trail bikes, built to survive big hits.

Downhill

Maximum travel and durability. For lift-accessed or shuttle runs. Not for climbing.

Cross Country (XC)

Lightweight and efficient. Built for climbing and racing. Less travel, faster pedaling.

All-Mountain

Versatile category between trail and enduro. Can climb reasonably well and handle descents.

Fork

The front suspension. Holds the front wheel and soaks up bumps before they reach your hands.

Shock

The rear suspension. Absorbs impacts for the back wheel. Sits between frame and rear wheel.
